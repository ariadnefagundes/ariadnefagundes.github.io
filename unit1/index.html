Taylor Swift’s Fight to Regain Ownership of Her Music

Taylor Swift’s battle to regain ownership of her music began in June 2019, when her former label, Big Machine Records, was sold to Scooter Braun, giving him control over the master recordings of her first six albums. Swift publicly objected, saying she was not given a fair chance to buy them herself.

In response, she began re-recording her albums in August 2019 to regain control. She released:

Re-Recordings:

  • "Fearless (Taylor’s Version)" – April 2021
  • "Red (Taylor’s Version)" – November 2021
  • "Speak Now (Taylor’s Version)" – July 2023
  • "1989 (Taylor’s Version)" – October 2023
In May 30, 2025 Taylor Swift officially bought back the master recordings and full rights to her first six albums from Shamrock Capital for approximately $360 million. By reclaiming her masters in May 2025, Swift achieved full ownership of her catalog—a landmark win for artists’ rights and a powerful demonstration of creative autonomy.

Taylor's letter letting fans know she now owns all of her work.

"I’m just so proud of the work that I’ve done. I’m so proud of the music that I made, and now I finally own it."

— Taylor Swift, 2021 interview with CBS Sunday Morning
